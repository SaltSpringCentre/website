Yoga Teacher Training

An immersive residential 200-hour program rooted in the teachings of Baba Hari Dass. Held over two intensive sessions spanning approximately 22 nights on-site, this training is led by 15+ multi-generational faculty members and carries a 4.91 out of 5 rating with Yoga Alliance.

The curriculum is grounded in classical eight-limbed Ashtanga and Hatha yoga. Studies include the Yoga Sutras of Patanjali, an introduction to Ayurveda, functional anatomy, pranayama, meditation, Shat Karma purification, kirtan, and mantra. Two tracks run in parallel: morning practice classes offered at multiple levels and styles, and afternoon clinics covering 28 core postures, student-teaching practica, alignment principles, props, and hands-on adjustments.

Faculty includes Chetna Tracy Boyd (Program Director, E-RYT 500, C-IAYT), Gita Tanya Roberts (E-RYT 500, C-IAYT), Yogeshwar Will Humphrey, and 12+ additional instructors drawn from the Centre's teaching lineage. Each brings decades of personal practice and a deep commitment to the tradition.

Tuition is $2,800 CAD. Accommodation and meals range from $1,870 to $5,390 depending on room type. Applications are currently closed. Sign up for the newsletter below to be notified when future openings become available.

Selfless Service

Karma Yoga Program

A live-in immersive experience blending service, spiritual practice, and community life. Karma Yogis dedicate 25 to 30 hours per week to the daily work of the Centre in exchange for accommodation, three vegetarian meals a day, and full access to all yoga classes, ceremonies, and community programs.

Three seasonal cohorts run each year: March through May, June through August, and September through December. Service areas include the kitchen, farm and gardens, grounds maintenance, housekeeping, building projects, and administration. Each area is essential to the functioning of this 50-year-old intentional community.

The philosophy is simple and profound: selfless service as taught by Baba Hari Dass. Actions performed without attachment to personal gain. Through daily work, shared meals, and practice alongside long-term residents, Karma Yogis discover a rhythm of living that is both grounding and transformative.

Qualified yoga teachers (200-hour certification or higher) may receive supervised teaching experience as part of their service. All Karma Yogis have access to trails, the swimming lake, community gardens, and the full weekly schedule of classes, satsang, and kirtan.

Satsang & Kirtan

Satsang means "union of truth." It is a gathering of seekers who come together to sing, chant, meditate, and hear spiritual readings. Kirtan is the practice of call-and-response devotional chanting, and it is central to the Centre's strong bhakti (devotion) tradition. These practices are open to everyone, regardless of background or belief.

Life at the Centre

Daily Rhythms

Life at the Salt Spring Centre of Yoga follows a rhythm shaped by decades of practice. Whether you are here for a weekend retreat or a season of Karma Yoga, each day unfolds with intention, community, and care.

Mornings begin with sadhana at 6:30 AM, followed by Arati at the temple. Breakfast is served communally, and the rest of the morning is given to classes, workshops, or service. A freshly prepared lunch gathers the community at midday. Afternoons bring asana practice, free time on the land, or continued study. Dinner is followed by evening offerings: satsang, kirtan, philosophy groups, or simply stillness under the stars.

All meals are lacto-vegetarian, freshly prepared on-site using produce from the Centre's own gardens wherever possible. Cooking follows sattvic principles, emphasizing purity, balance, and nourishment. Food here is not separate from the practice. It is part of it.

The Centre is home to three temples: the Hanuman Temple, the Ganesh Temple, and the Holy Mother/Garden Temple. Ceremonies, pujas, and yajnas are held regularly and are open to all visitors. Participation is always optional and never mandatory. The temples and their ceremonies simply hold the space, offering a quiet anchor for those who wish to connect.
