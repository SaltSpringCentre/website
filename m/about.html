A Rare and Sacred Place

The Salt Spring Centre of Yoga is a non-profit educational and spiritual retreat centre dedicated to teaching the core principles of yoga: peace, resilience, and belonging. Founded by master yogi Baba Hari Dass, the Centre is the west coast of Canada's longest running spiritual and wellness retreat centre.

Established in 1981, the Centre serves as the primary initiative of the Dharma Sara Satsang Society (founded 1974). For over four decades, thousands have experienced transformation through immersive programs ranging from weekend retreats to full-time karma yoga residencies.

Our Vision

All people can attain peace, resilience, and belonging.

Our Mission

The Salt Spring Centre of Yoga offers the principles and practices of classical yoga for personal development within a peaceful, positive environment.

Baba Hari Dass (1923-2018)

Our Founder and Teacher

Baba Hari Dass, affectionately called Babaji, was a master yogi, scholar, and spiritual teacher. He possessed remarkable abilities in sculpture, music, architecture, yogic philosophy, writing, storytelling, martial arts, and Indian cosmology. He was a leading proponent of Ayurveda and authored numerous books.

Babaji was a mauni sadhu, having undertaken a vow of silence in 1952. He never spoke another audible word, communicating through a small chalkboard worn around his neck. He had an extraordinary gift for distilling complex teachings into their essential form.

His life's aim centered on bringing people to Sadhana, a daily spiritual practice encompassing positive qualities, right conduct, family relationships, ethical livelihood, and inner peace.

Connection to Western Yoga

Babaji was a formative teacher of Ram Dass, influencing his seminal 1971 work Be Here Now. In 1970, he met American students near Haridwar, India, including Anand Dass (AD), a Canadian who would establish yoga practice in Vancouver.

Founding the Centre

Following Babaji's 1974 visit to Canada, the Dharma Sara Satsang Society was established. After successful annual retreats, the group purchased a heritage farmstead on Salt Spring Island in 1981, creating the Salt Spring Centre of Yoga.

Sri Ram Ashram

Honouring a childhood vow, Babaji established Sri Ram Ashram near Haridwar in 1984 using publishing revenue. This orphanage provides loving care and educational support for abandoned children.

Legacy

Babaji left his body peacefully on September 25, 2018. His influence persists through the institutions he inspired, his writings, and all those whose hearts he touched. His autobiography, The Path Unfolds, documents his life experiences.

"Love everyone, including yourself. This is real Sadhana."

69 Acres of Living Sanctuary

The Land

The 69-acre property encompasses diverse natural ecosystems including old growth cedars, maples, and alders. The land includes an organic farm, fruit orchard, and permaculture food forest cultivating nuts, medicinal herbs, and fruit.

The property borders the Salt Spring Island Conservancy's Blackburn Lake nature reserve, which hosts over 110 species, many endangered or vulnerable.

The Dandaka Forest, located on the Centre's property, is one of the most untouched, primordial cedar-swamp wetlands found in North America.

Dharma Sara Satsang Society

A Non-Profit Legacy of Yoga and Service. The name signifies 'the essence of right living' (Dharma Sara) and 'gathering with people in truth' (Satsang). Members unite around yoga practices taught by Baba Hari Dass.

Inspired by Babaji's teachings, the Society promotes Classical Ashtanga Yoga through spiritual practice (Sadhana), selfless service (Karma Yoga), and community support (Satsang).

The Centre envisions itself as a thriving spiritual community offering advanced instruction while maintaining sustainable operations for future generations.

Gulf Islands, BC

Salt Spring Island

Salt Spring Island, the most populous of British Columbia's Gulf Islands, is located between the Lower Mainland and Vancouver Island. The island attracts approximately 11,000 permanent residents, increasing to over 30,000 during peak season.

Known for its agricultural heritage, creative communities, and eco-conscious culture, the island features farm stands, native wildlife, ocean access, and freshwater lakes for swimming.

Getting Here

Salt Spring Island is accessible by BC Ferries from Tsawwassen (Lower Mainland), Swartz Bay (Victoria), and Crofton (Duncan). Harbour Air offers float plane service from Vancouver.

The Centre is located at 355 Blackburn Road, a 10-minute drive from Ganges village.
