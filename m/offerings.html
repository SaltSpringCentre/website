Yoga Teacher Training

An immersive residential 200-hour program rooted in the teachings of Baba Hari Dass. Held over two intensive sessions spanning approximately 22 nights on-site, this training is led by 15+ multi-generational faculty members and carries a 4.91 out of 5 rating with Yoga Alliance.

The curriculum is grounded in classical eight-limbed Ashtanga and Hatha yoga. Studies include the Yoga Sutras of Patanjali, an introduction to Ayurveda, functional anatomy, pranayama, meditation, Shat Karma purification, kirtan, and mantra.

Faculty includes Chetna Tracy Boyd (Program Director, E-RYT 500, C-IAYT), Gita Tanya Roberts (E-RYT 500, C-IAYT), Yogeshwar Will Humphrey, and 12+ additional instructors.

Tuition is $2,800 CAD. Accommodation and meals range from $1,870 to $5,390 depending on room type. Applications are currently closed.

Selfless Service

Karma Yoga Program

A live-in immersive experience blending service, spiritual practice, and community life. Karma Yogis dedicate 25 to 30 hours per week to the daily work of the Centre in exchange for accommodation, three vegetarian meals a day, and full access to all yoga classes, ceremonies, and community programs.

Three seasonal cohorts run each year: March through May, June through August, and September through December. Service areas include the kitchen, farm and gardens, grounds maintenance, housekeeping, building projects, and administration.

The philosophy is simple and profound: selfless service as taught by Baba Hari Dass. Actions performed without attachment to personal gain.

Life at the Centre

Daily Rhythms

Life at the Salt Spring Centre of Yoga follows a rhythm shaped by decades of practice. Whether you are here for a weekend retreat or a season of Karma Yoga, each day unfolds with intention, community, and care.

Mornings begin with sadhana at 6:30 AM, followed by Arati at the temple. Breakfast is served communally, and the rest of the morning is given to classes, workshops, or service. A freshly prepared lunch gathers the community at midday. Afternoons bring asana practice, free time on the land, or continued study.

All meals are lacto-vegetarian, freshly prepared on-site using produce from the Centre's own gardens wherever possible. Cooking follows sattvic principles, emphasizing purity, balance, and nourishment.

The Centre is home to three temples: the Hanuman Temple, the Ganesh Temple, and the Holy Mother/Garden Temple. Ceremonies, pujas, and yajnas are held regularly and are open to all visitors.
