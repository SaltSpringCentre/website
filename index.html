A Rare and Sacred Place

The Salt Spring Centre of Yoga is a non-profit retreat centre dedicated to teaching the core principles of yoga: peace, resilience, and belonging.

Founded by venerated master yogi Baba Hari Dass, the Centre is the west coast of Canada's longest running spiritual and wellness retreat centre. Here, the magic of 69 acres weaves together with the stories of those transformed by it.

Whether you're here for a weekend retreat, a yoga immersion, or a full-time karma exchange, the Centre becomes the container you need for deep, challenging, and playful personal work.

"Work honestly, meditate every day, meet people without fear, and play."

Baba Hari Dass

The Land

The 69-acre property sits in a spectacular natural setting of cedars, maples, alders, and cottonwoods, home to over 110 species, many endangered or vulnerable.

Walking the land you'll find a large organic farm, an abundant fruit orchard, a permaculture food forest growing nuts, medicinal herbs, and fruit, and beautiful streams and ponds.

The Centre also contains the Dandaka Forest, one of the most untouched, primordial cedar-swamp wetlands found in North America.

Our Founder

Baba Hari Dass

Babaji was a master yogi and scholar with remarkable skill in sculpture, music, architecture, yogic philosophy, writing, and storytelling. He was a leading proponent of Ayurveda and author of many books.

A mauni sadhu, he undertook a vow of continual silence in 1952 and never spoke another word aloud. He communicated by means of a small chalkboard worn around his neck, with the marvelous skill of conveying a great deal in the fewest possible words.

Babaji left his body on September 25, 2018. His wisdom, good works, and inspiration live on in the institutions he created, his writings, and all those he touched.
